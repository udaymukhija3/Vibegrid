# VibeGrid Product Vision

## Ratified Vision

VibeGrid is a daily semantic grouping puzzle where players sort 16 tiles into 4 hidden vibe-based categories. It should be quick to finish, funny enough to forward, and editorially sharper than a generic word association game.

The core promise is a 2-5 minute ritual: open the grid, argue with yourself, find the vibe, share the result without spoiling the answer.

## Product Pillars

1. Fast ritual: one daily puzzle, no account required, low friction.
2. Editorial taste: category names should feel authored, cultural, and a little dangerous.
3. Spoiler-safe sharing: result text should invite others in without revealing groups.
4. Trustworthy game state: refreshes, duplicate guesses, and racing submits should not corrupt an attempt.
5. Small product surface: puzzle first, admin second, identity later.

## MVP Definition

- Today route with one published puzzle.
- 16-tile board.
- Select exactly 4 tiles.
- Server validates guesses.
- Correct groups lock and reveal their category name.
- Incorrect guesses increment mistake count.
- 4 mistakes ends the attempt.
- Completed result shows time, mistakes, solved groups, and share text.
- Anonymous attempt persists across refreshes.
- Seeded puzzle content exists before admin tooling is built.

## Voice

VibeGrid should sound like it came from a person with taste, not a template. Category names can be playful, internet-native, regionally specific, or lightly absurd, but they should still be fair once revealed.

Good target tone:

- Corporate séance
- Soft launch of a personality
- Group chat currently on fire
- Airport personality collapse
- Premium mediocrity

Avoid:

- Generic category labels that could appear in any word game.
- Punching down at people or identities.
- References so obscure that solving becomes trivia instead of semantic grouping.
